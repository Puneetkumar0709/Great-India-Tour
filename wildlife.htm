Wildlife Safaris: Into the Wild

Track the Royal Bengal Tiger, spot the One-Horned Rhino, and explore diverse Indian jungles.

Ranthambore National Park

Famous for its tiger population and historical fort, Ranthambore offers one of the best chances to see a tiger in daylight.

Location: Rajasthan
Budget: ₹35,000 - ₹60,000
Transportation: Train to Sawai Madhopur, then Cab
Sites: Tiger Safaris, Ranthambore Fort, Padam Talao

Jim Corbett National Park

India's oldest national park, known for its picturesque landscape, dense forests, and elephant herds alongside tigers.

Location: Uttarakhand (Himalayan Foothills)
Budget: ₹30,000 - ₹55,000
Transportation: Train to Ramnagar, then Cab
Sites: Dhikala Zone, Bijrani Safari, Kosi River

Bandhavgarh National Park

Boasts the highest density of the Royal Bengal Tiger population in the world, set against a backdrop of ancient ruins.

Location: Madhya Pradesh (Central India)
Budget: ₹40,000 - ₹70,000
Transportation: Flight to Jabalpur (JLR), then Cab
Sites: Tala Zone, Bandhavgarh Fort, Sheshya Sai Statue

Gir Forest National Park

The only place in the world where the majestic Asiatic Lion can be seen in its natural habitat.

Location: Gujarat (West India)
Budget: ₹30,000 - ₹50,000
Transportation: Flight to Diu (DIU) or Rajkot, then Cab
Sites: Devalia Safari Park, Sasan Gir, Kamleshwar Dam

Kaziranga National Park

A UNESCO World Heritage Site famous for protecting two-thirds of the world's population of the Great One-Horned Rhinoceros.

Location: Assam (Northeast India)
Budget: ₹45,000 - ₹75,000
Transportation: Flight to Jorhat/Guwahati, then Cab/Bus
Sites: Central/Western Range Safari, Elephant Safaris
